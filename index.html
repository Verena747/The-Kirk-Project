Frequently Asked Questions

The Kirk Project is an initiative focused on responsibly identifying and preventing potential acts of targeted, political, and community violence through public reporting and professional assessment.

Anyone who observes a potential threat or concerning behavior — whether online or in person — can submit a report. Community members, educators, law enforcement, and the general public are all encouraged to participate.

Reports are reviewed by trained analysts who evaluate the credibility, context, and potential risk of each submission. If necessary, information is referred to appropriate authorities or mental health professionals.

Yes. You may choose to remain anonymous when submitting a report. However, providing contact information can help investigators follow up for additional details if needed.

Reports may include threats related to schools, workplaces, politics, communities, or individuals. Even offhand or emotional comments on social media can be assessed to ensure public safety.

The Kirk Project works independently but collaborates with law enforcement and mental health professionals when credible threats are identified or when safety concerns arise.

False or malicious reports are carefully documented and may be subject to review. Intentional misuse of the reporting system undermines community safety and may carry consequences.

All reports are handled with strict confidentiality. Personally identifiable information is protected, and data is shared only when necessary for credible threat assessment.

Many acts of violence show clear warning signs beforehand. By taking every threat seriously — even small ones — The Kirk Project helps prevent harm before it occurs.

You can support The Kirk Project by spreading awareness, volunteering, or participating in community safety initiatives. Every vigilant citizen plays a role in prevention.
